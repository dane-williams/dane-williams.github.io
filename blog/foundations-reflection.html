    • Final Foundation Reflections


    Three things ive learnt about myself:

    I can shut down when i'm overwhelmed. I dont feel like talking and can be quite grumpy which is something i'll obviously need to work on.

    Theres a huge difference in clarity between my 'fresh mind' and my 'tired mind'. There were several moments where i'd struggle with something for a very long time but after a small break the answer would come to me.

    I definitely learn better by doing.


    The role of VALUES, EMPATHY and SELF AWARENESS in learning and programming:

    In a nutshell it's about being good to yourself and to others. As stated above. Ive come to realise I work vastly better when i'm fresh and focused. Knowing this means i'll know that taking a quick break will save time compared to pushing through in a fog.
    You need to be aware of your failings so you can mitigate and improve them whilst also leaning into your strengths.
    Generally in life and so in programming you will work collaboratively with others. Being aware of yourself and the needs of your collaborators will make things run more smoothly.


    What suprised you the most about the core learning:

    Working in physical jobs for most of my life there was a tendency to just push through. Its been a struggle to put that mentality aside. This core has shown me that its a lot harder to do when doing things that utilise your mental faculties more.


    Why Dev concentrates on this:

    Being more aware of yourself and others makes you a better learner, a better person and a better employee. So its a very important skill to learn.


    Was it worth it:

    Programming skills will come with time and practice. The core skills learnt in foundations however will increase the speed at which I will learn carrying on into the future and will make me more employable and a better team member. The most useful skill its taught me is a much better awareness of my potential failings so that I can improve these.
